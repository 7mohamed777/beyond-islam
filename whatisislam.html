üåü Islam: More Than Just a Religion

The word "Islam" derives from the Arabic root S-L-M, meaning peace, purity, and submission. A Muslim is one who submits to Allah's will, finding peace in this life and the hereafter through complete devotion.

Islam: The Natural Way

Islam is considered the natural religion (fitrah) - the innate inclination every human is born with to recognize and worship their Creator. It completes and perfects the monotheistic message revealed to all prophets.

Key Understanding:

Islam integrates spirituality with practical life - there's no separation between "religious" and "secular" matters. Every action, when done with the right intention, becomes an act of worship.

Qur'an: The Living Miracle

The Qur'an is the literal word of Allah, revealed to Prophet Muhammad (S.A.W) over 23 years. It remains unchanged in its original Arabic text, containing guidance for all aspects of life.

The Sunnah in Practice:

The Sunnah includes everything Prophet Muhammad (S.A.W) said, did, or approved. It provides practical examples of how to implement Qur'anic teachings in daily life, from prayer to business ethics.

2

Five daily prayers connecting Muslims with Allah throughout the day

3

4

Fasting during Ramadan from dawn to sunset for spiritual purification

5
Hajj

Pilgrimage to Mecca once in lifetime for those physically and financially able

Core Ethical Principles:
  • Justice (Adl): Fairness in all matters, even against oneself
  • Mercy (Rahma): Compassion toward all living beings
  • Honor (Ihsan): Excellence in all actions
  • Trustworthiness (Amanah): Reliability in responsibilities
  • Moderation (Wasatiyyah): Balanced approach to life
  • Patience (Sabr): Perseverance through difficulties
  • Gratitude (Shukr): Thankfulness for blessings

üåç Islamic Society and Human Rights

Islam establishes clear rights and responsibilities for individuals, families, and communities, promoting social harmony and justice.

Key Social Principles
  • Equality: All humans equal before Allah regardless of race, ethnicity, or social status
  • Women's Rights: Right to education, inheritance, property ownership, and consent in marriage
  • Family Values: Strong emphasis on family ties, parental respect, and marital harmony
  • Community Welfare: Collective responsibility to support the poor, orphans, and vulnerable
  • Environmental Stewardship: Responsibility to protect and preserve natural resources
